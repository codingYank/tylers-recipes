Crusty French Baguette

Ingredients

  • 1.75 ounces warm water
  • 1.5 tsp active dry yeast
  • 16 ounces bread flour
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • 10 ounces cool water
  • additional flour for dusting

Instructions

  • Add yeast to the warm water and allow to dissolve
  • Place the measure bread flour into a large bowl and stir in the salt
  • Make a well in the center of the flour mixture and stir in the dissolved yeast
  • Add in the cool water while stirring until a stiff shaggy dough forms (you may not need all the water)
  • Cover bowl with plastic wrap and let rest for 30 minutes
  • Transfer dought to lightly flowered work surface, and gently press it into a rectagle and fold into thirds. Turn 90 degrees and repeat
  • Place dought in a large oiled bowl and cover woth plastic wrap. Allow it to rise for in a warm place until doubled in size (1 - 2 hours)
  • Divide dought into 4 equal parts and shape each one into a loaf 15 inches long and 1.5 inches in diameter
  • Place loaves on floured towel, cover with oiled plastic wrap, and allow to rise until doubled in size (30 - 45 minutes)
  • Preheat oven to 450 degrees F and place a pan of water on the bottom rack
  • Uncover the baguettes and transfer to a lightly greased baking sheet
  • Sprinkle with flour and make 4 elongated clashes down each one with a sharp knife
  • Bake bread until crusty and brown (around 30 minutes, bread should sound hollow when done)
